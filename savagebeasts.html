Savage Beasts: The Spatial Conflict Between Civilization and Wilderness in The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim

In The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim, players encounter a vast virtual environment, populated by numerous and diverse creatures. The virtual space of the game, however, is not homogeneous. Various aspects of its representation and structure creates a division between urban space, or civilization, and the non-urban natural environment, or wilderness. The differences between these two spaces, both in terms of their representation and their structural implementation, create a world in which the natural environment is seen as threateningly hostile, a space that must be overcome and dominated; this process is not only integral to a playthrough of Skyrim, but serves as an indicator of the playerâ€™s progress while they play. By interrogating the construction of this spatial division of the game, it becomes apparent that this paradigm of civilization versus wilderness pervades the design of Skyrim and creates an extremely problematic relationship between the player and their virtual environment. This is all the more apparent in light of the issues we currently face in terms of our own environment, from mass extinction to global climate change.
